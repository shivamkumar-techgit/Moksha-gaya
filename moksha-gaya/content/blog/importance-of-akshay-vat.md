## Overview
No ancestral ritual (Pind Daan) in the holy city of Gaya is considered complete without visiting **Akshay Vat**—the immortal Banyan tree. Located near the banks of the Falgu River, this ancient tree represents eternity, continuity, and divine presence. Devotees believe that offering the final Pind (rice ball) under the branches of Akshay Vat satisfies ancestors permanently, securing their final liberation (*Moksha*).

## Importance
The word *Akshay* means imperishable or eternal, and *Vat* means Banyan tree. 
According to legend, when **Goddess Sita** accompanied Lord Rama and Lakshmana to Gaya for the Pind Daan of King Dasharatha, Lord Rama went to collect puja materials. As the auspicious time (*muhurta*) was passing, the spirit of Dasharatha appeared and demanded the offering. Sita, under pressure, performed Pind Daan using Falgu sand (*Pindas*), witnessed by the Falgu River, a cow, a tulsi plant, a Brahmana, and the Akshay Vat tree. 
When Lord Rama returned and questioned her, the first four witnesses lied out of greed, denying the ritual. Only the **Akshay Vat tree spoke the truth**, testifying that Sita had indeed performed the rites. Sita cursed the first four witnesses (which is why the Falgu River runs dry and Brahmanas in Gaya are never fully satisfied), but she **blessed the Akshay Vat tree with immortality**, declaring it would never lose its leaves or die, and that all Gaya Shraddha rites must culminate under its canopy.

## Benefits
* **Eternal Ancestral Satisfaction**: Sita's blessing ensures that any offering made under the Akshay Vat tree feeds and satisfies ancestors for eternity.
* **Lineage Blessings**: Performs the final sealing of ancestral debt, ensuring that the blessings of the Pitrus flow uninterrupted to the performer's family.
* **Immortality of Rites**: The imperishable nature of the tree ensures that the spiritual merit (*Punya*) of your Gaya visit remains forever with your soul.

## Procedure
1. **Temple Visits**: Perform initial Pind Daan at Falgu River and Vishnupad Temple.
2. **Transfer to Akshay Vat**: Travel to the Akshay Vat temple compound.
3. **Offerings**: The Pandit guides you to place the final Pindas under the tree trunk.
4. **Vow of Abstinence**: Perform the *Sita-Sankalp*. Per traditional custom, devotees vow to give up one specific food, habit, or desire forever under the tree, symbolizing detachment and sacrifice.
5. **Aarti**: Perform parikrama (circumambulation) of the tree and make prayers.

## Best Time
* **Pitru Paksha**: Highly auspicious period in September-October when thousands of devotees gather under the tree daily.
* **Somvati Amavasya**: New moon days falling on Mondays are considered exceptionally powerful for Akshay Vat worship.

## FAQs
### Q: Why is the Akshay Vat tree considered immortal?
A: According to scriptural legend, Goddess Sita blessed the tree with immortality for testifying to her honesty. Even during geological changes, the tree has survived and never sheds its canopy fully.

### Q: What food or habit should I give up under the tree?
A: You can choose to give up any favorite food (like a specific fruit, vegetable, or sweet) or a negative habit (like anger, gossip, or smoking) as a sacrifice to the ancestors.

### Q: Can we perform Pind Daan only at Akshay Vat?
A: No. Under Gaya Shraddha guidelines, Akshay Vat is the final step. Rituals must begin at the Falgu River and Vishnupad Temple first.

### Q: Is there an entrance fee for Akshay Vat?
A: There is no formal entry fee. However, local temple priests coordinate the tree-side Pujas and require nominal materials cost or optional dakshina.

### Q: How old is the Akshay Vat tree?
A: The tree has been recorded in ancient scriptures dating back thousands of years. It is geographically and historically verified as one of the oldest living spiritual trees in India.
