## Why is Narayan Bali Puja Performed?

The name *Narayan Bali* literally translates to:
*   **Narayan:** Referring to Lord Vishnu, the protector of the universe.
*   **Bali:** Referring to a sacred offering or sacrifice.

Unlike standard Shradh, which satisfies ancestors who died naturally, Narayan Bali is performed to appease Lord Vishnu and request his personal intervention to release a soul trapped due to unnatural or sudden circumstances.

### Untimely and Unnatural Deaths:
According to the *Garuda Purana*, a soul enters the restless spirit state (*Preta*) if death occurs due to:
*   Accidents (road, rail, fire, or water)
*   Suicide or self-harm
*   Violence, murder, or weapons
*   Sudden, shocking illnesses before completing natural life stages
*   Snakebite or poisoning

Because these souls pass away with intense shock and unfulfilled desires, they cannot cross over to *Pitru Loka* on their own. They remain in the earthly atmosphere, causing spiritual friction that manifests in the lives of their living descendants.

### Severe Astrological Pitru Dosha:
In Vedic astrology, Narayan Bali is the mandatory remedy when the Sun or Moon (representing parents and soul) is conjunct with Rahu or Ketu in the 1st, 5th, or 9th houses of the birth horoscope, indicating deep ancestral debt.

## Step-by-Step Procedure: The 2-Day Vedic Ceremony

The Narayan Bali Puja is a highly structured, 2-day ceremony conducted by a team of Vedic scholars under the supervision of a Gayawal Panda.

### Day 1: The Vow, Purificatory Baths and Artificial Body Creation

1.  **Sacred Bath and Sankalp:** The Karta (devotee) takes a purificatory bath at the Falgu River and sits for the *Sankalp* (sacred vow), declaring their intent to perform the Narayan Bali for the peace of their ancestors.
2.  **Creation of the Artificial Body:** Since the physical body of the deceased is gone, the priests construct a small symbolic body using wheat flour or Kush grass, placing it on a copper plate. This body acts as the temporary vehicle for the ritual.
3.  **Appeasing the Pancha Devatas:** Ritual prayers are offered to five deities — Lord Vishnu, Lord Shiva, Lord Brahma, Yamraj, and the Ancestral Deity (*Pitru Devata*).
4.  **Narayan Bali Havan:** A sacred fire ceremony (*Havan*) is performed. Specialized Vedic mantras from the *Yajurveda* are chanted, requesting Lord Vishnu to accept the soul and dissolve its negative karmic records.
5.  **Pinda Arpan and Visarjan:** At the end of Day 1, the symbolic body is cremated or dissolved in the water, signifying the release of the spirit from its physical attachments.

### Day 2: The Concluding Shradh and Brahmin Bhojan

1.  **Sapindikarana Shraddha:** The second day begins with the *Sapindikarana* — a ritual that merges the individual soul (*Preta*) with the collective ancestors (*Pitrus*).
2.  **Vishnupad Footprint Arpan:** Devotees carry the final Pindas to the inner sanctum of the Vishnupad Temple, placing them directly on Lord Vishnu's footprint to seal the liberation.
3.  **Feeding the Brahmins (Brahmin Bhojan):** A minimum of 5 to 11 Vedic Brahmins are fed a traditional satvik meal. Offering Dakshina (cash charity) and new white garments to the priests completes the ritual.
