## About the Service
Many devotees residing abroad (NRIs) or facing physical, health, or travel constraints are unable to visit the sacred city of Gaya in person. However, performing ancestral rites is a non-negotiable spiritual duty. To bridge this gap, Moksha Gaya offers a premium **Online Pind Daan Service**.

This service allows you to participate in the entire ritual remotely via high-definition live video. A verified, experienced Pandit in Gaya performs the physical offerings on your behalf at the sacred spots, while you take the vows (Sankalp) virtually from your home.

---

## Spiritual Significance
In Vedic tradition, *Sankalp* (intent and resolution) is the most critical element of any ritual. During the Online Pind Daan, the Pandit guides you to take the Sankalp using your name, gotra, and lineage details. 

Once the Sankalp is made, the physical offerings (Pind and Tarpan) performed by the priest at the holy spots of Gaya (like the Falgu River or Vishnupad Temple) are scripturally valid and accepted by the ancestors. The distance does not diminish the spiritual merit when the intent is pure and the Vedic procedures are followed precisely.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Q: Is Online Pind Daan scripturally valid?
A: Yes. Hindu scriptures support performing rituals through a representative (called *Pratinidhi*) when physical presence is not possible. The Sankalp is taken in your name, making the ritual spiritually effective.

### Q: What do I need to prepare at home for the live session?
A: You only need a stable internet connection, a quiet space, and a copper vessel with fresh water, flowers, and some sesame seeds (optional) to take the virtual Sankalp along with the Pandit.

### Q: How is the Prasad sent to international addresses?
A: We pack the sacred dry Prasad (non-perishable) securely and ship it via international courier partners. Delivery times vary by country.
