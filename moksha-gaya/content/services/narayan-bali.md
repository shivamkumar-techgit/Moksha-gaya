## About the Service
Narayan Bali is a highly specialized and powerful Vedic ritual performed to liberate souls who died prematurely, unnaturally, or under tragic circumstances (such as accidents, suicides, water, fire, or sudden illness). If these souls do not receive proper transit rites, they may remain trapped in the earthly realm, causing *Pitru Dosha* (ancestral afflictions) in the family.

Gaya Rituals organizes Narayan Bali rituals in accordance with the Garuda Purana scriptures. The ceremony is performed by specially trained Vedic pandits in Gaya, focusing on seeking forgiveness from Lord Vishnu to free the departed souls.

## Spiritual Significance
The name *Narayan Bali* means offering a sacrifice (*bali*) to Lord Narayan (Vishnu). Since Lord Vishnu is the ultimate savior and liberator of all souls, this ritual is performed to appeal to Him directly. By establishing a symbolic connection with Lord Vishnu, the troubled soul is purified, freed from its suffering, and granted passage to higher spiritual realms.

Performing Narayan Bali helps resolve:
* Frequent obstacles in family growth and prosperity.
* Delays in marriage or difficulties in conceiving children (progeny issues).
* Unresolved emotional heaviness or sudden health crises in the family.
* Severe horoscope afflictions related to Pitru Dosha.

## Process Overview
*This is a detailed ritual that typically takes place over two days.*

### Day 1:
1. **Sankalp & Purification**: The performer undergoes body purification rites and takes the solemn vow.
2. **Deity Worship**: Invoking and worshipping Lord Vishnu, Lord Shiva, and Lord Yama.
3. **Symbolic Funeral Rites**: Since the soul did not receive proper rites at death, a symbolic funeral ceremony is performed using wheat flour figures.
4. **Narayan Bali Havan**: Performing a special fire sacrifice using Vedic mantras to invoke the mercy of Lord Vishnu.

### Day 2:
5. **Pind Daan & Tarpan**: Offering 16 custom Pinds and performing Tarpan to settle the departed soul.
6. **Brahman Bhoj**: Feeding Vedic pandits and donating clothes, grains, and dakshina to complete the vow.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Q: Why is Narayan Bali performed?
A: It is performed when family members face persistent challenges, or when there is a known case of untimely or accidental death in the family lineage, indicating that the departed soul is not at peace.

### Q: Can it be performed at any time of the year?
A: Narayan Bali is performed on specific auspicious tithis (days) recommended by our astrologers. It is commonly performed during Pitru Paksha, in the months of Kartik, Margashirsha, or during auspicious transits.

### Q: Who must perform this ritual?
A: It is performed by the head of the family, a son, or a close male relative. In cases where only female members are present, they can get the ritual performed through our pandits under custom guidelines.
